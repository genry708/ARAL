Why We Advocate

Education is a fundamental right, that every child deserves. It provides the foundation for learning, growth, and personal development, opening doors to opportunities that help learners reach their full potential. Yet millions of children around the world‚Äîespecially those who are in rural and marginalized communities‚Äîare still denied access to quality learning. In the Philippines, many students face overcrowded classrooms, long commutes to schools, and a lack of textbooks and facilities. Rural and marginalized communities are often the most affected, with children struggling to keep up due to poverty and limited resources.

Around the world, children face similar challenges in accessing quality education. Ensuring inclusive and equitable learning opportunities globally is essential so that every learner, regardless of where they are, can thrive and achieve their dreams.

Current Education Efforts

The Department of Education (DepEd) has introduced the Quality Basic Education Development Plan 2025‚Äì2035, a roadmap designed to reform the current education system. This includes revising the national curriculum, building 40,000 new classrooms through public‚Äìprivate partnerships, and implementing the ARAL program to help struggling learners catch up. Initiatives to improve digital learning, such as the DepEd Computerization Program, are also being rolled out to provide laptops and smart TVs to public schools.

Teacher training is also being prioritized, with the National Educators Academy of the Philippines (NEAP) and PEAC offering professional development programs. These aim to enhance subject mastery, digital skills, and inclusive teaching methods. Such efforts recognize that quality education depends not only on classrooms and textbooks, but also on well-trained, motivated teachers.

ARAL in Action

This is where ARAL steps in.
